Must-see attractions for your itinerary

Immerse in Yala, Sri Lanka's second vastest park. Lush greenery teems with wildlife, while whispers of past civilizations linger in ancient ruins. Embark on a safari - spot unique creatures and explore history's footprints. Uncover the wild within

Sigiriya's iconic Rock Fortress reigns over Sri Lanka's center, with nearby Dambulla's cave temples and Pidurangala's scenic climbs adding ancient whispers and breathtaking views.

Arugam Bay's crescent beach is paradise, perfect for surfing or simply letting time slip away under swaying palms. While fishermen work diligently by the south end, evenings bring locals together for chat and a tranquil paddle. Come for the waves, stay for the soul-soothing vibes.

Horton Plains soars between mighty peaks, boasting jaw-dropping World's End cliffs and a whispering waterfall. Packed with diverse life, rivers, and hidden gems, this highland park brims with magic.

For three centuries Polonnaruwa was a royal capital of both the Chola and Sinhalese kingdoms. Today's archaeological park is a delight to explore, with hundreds of ancient structures – tombs and temples, statues and stupas – in a sprawling forested core.

Whale and dolphin watching in Mirissa is one of the most exciting water activities you can do during your holiday in Sri Lanka.

Built in 1921, the Nine Arches Bridge, or "Bridge in the Sky," soared from local bridge to Instagram darling, becoming Ella's most iconic landmark. This colonial masterpiece now draws crowds eager to capture its grandeur amidst lush Sri Lankan hills.

Soaring 2,243 meters in Sri Lanka's heart, Adam's Peak isn't just the island's seventh tallest mountain. It's a sacred icon, worshipped by all faiths for the footprint it bears atop. This majestic peak draws pilgrims and trekkers alike, captivated by its beauty and challenging climb.

80km from Colombo, Udawalawe Park, Sri Lanka's 6th wildlife gem, awaits! Join herds of elephants, buffalo, deer, and monkeys on a jeep safari. Leopards are shy, but cameras ready for all!

In Dambulla, Sri Lanka, the Dambulla Cave Temples reign supreme. Not just sacred and historic, these wonders hold an irresistible secret: they dwell within caves, drawing tourists seeking an awe-inspiring blend of nature and devotion.

The Koggala Sea Turtle farm rescues injured turtles and buys poached eggs, offering sanctuary and releasing hatchlings. While hands-on activities like touching raise concerns, dedicated staff share their knowledge about these amazing creatures.

Soaring 129 meters, Lakshapana Ella (or Laxapana Falls) ranks among Sri Lanka's top ten waterfalls in height. Just 9km west of Norton Bridge, this majestic horsetail fall awaits near Castlereigh Reservoir.
